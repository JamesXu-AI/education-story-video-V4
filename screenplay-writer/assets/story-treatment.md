## Segment and Seedance Strategy

<First define complete Scenes by primary time, place, and enclosing dramatic event;
then design the strongest continuous dramatic, performance, blocking/facing, and
editorial flow before using the fewest performable 4–15 second Segments. A Segment
or explicit serial Segment chain must preserve the complete micro-plot or conflict
rather than isolate one line, reaction, action wave, or future camera cut. Allow
independent execution only at a genuine Scene/time/place/enclosing-event change.
Classify every retained adjacent same-Scene boundary as serial soft
first-frame-reference after a settled/motivated cut, or serial predecessor-video
reference for unfinished visual/performance/camera motion. The first is an ordinary
reference-image continuity cue, never a strict/API first-frame lock. Pairwise-audit
adjacent same-Scene Segments and merge them whenever the combined dramatic unit fits one
compatible generation task. Do not pad runtime with static profiles, empty stares,
repeated breathing, redundant reactions, settled poses, or unmotivated silence;
every sustained hold must actively change dramatic meaning. Preserve story-critical
action phase, entrance/contact, walking direction, facing, eyelines, blocking, and
camera phase when the serial successor must inherit them. Actual visual media remains
downstream-owned and carries no source dialogue, lips, or audio.>
