<First define complete Scenes by primary time, place, and enclosing dramatic event;
then use the fewest performable 4–15 second Segments. Each Segment must express a
complete micro-plot or micro-conflict—established situation/pressure, dramatic need,

one line, reaction, action wave, or future camera cut. Pairwise-audit adjacent
same-Scene Segments and merge them whenever the combined dramatic unit fits one
compatible generation task. Do not pad runtime with static profiles, empty stares,
repeated breathing, redundant reactions, settled poses, or unmotivated silence;
every sustained hold must actively change dramatic meaning. Any optional visual
continuity input remains reference-only and carries no source dialogue, lips, or
