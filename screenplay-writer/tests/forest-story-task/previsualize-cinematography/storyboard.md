# Cinematic Storyboard: In the Forest

## Project Direction

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Format and Runtime | 16:9 widescreen, 24 fps, 157 seconds, English synchronized native dialogue. |
| Audience Point of View | Begin beside Uthman at child eye height; enter the fable as a witness among the forest community; end beside Uthman after he reaches the lesson himself. |
| Visual Progression | Warm, balanced sitting-room frames give way to a forest organized around Lion's imposed vertical power. The clearing gradually changes from an empty center surrounded by retreating groups to a community-controlled ring around a fallen ruler. |
| Camera Grammar | The room uses restrained locked frames and short motivated pushes. The forest uses low angles only while Lion controls height, lateral or crowd-level views for challengers, precise close views for injury and reaction, and wider level compositions as collective agency returns. One primary move per internal Shot. |
| Lighting and Color | The room uses amber practical light against quiet blue evening fill. The Eid clearing begins in soft green-gold daylight; Lion's throne carries denser ochre and shadow; the palette opens and regains green after his defeat. Blood remains red and readable but occupies little frame area. |
| Production Design | The low table, cup, book, root throne, central path, left trail, three tree gaps, Eid cloths, food baskets, prey-skin mantle, and feather crown remain fixed spatial anchors. |
| Performance Grammar | Power is shown through who occupies the center, who controls another's gaze, and whether a movement reaches its landing. Entrances retain trigger, route, first visibility, final mark, and witness response. Silent listeners keep mouths closed and react through breath, eyes, weight, and spacing. |
| Injury and Consequence | Elephant's bite and three bleeding shoulder scratches are directly visible; Zebra's fatal impact, bleeding lip, last breath, and death are unambiguous; Lion's swollen bleeding eyelid, bruised ribs, and scratched foreleg remain clear without exposed tissue or gore. |
| Native Audio | Room tone, clock, cup, forest ambience, footsteps, roar, buzz, impacts, breath, music entries and stops, and exact dialogue are recorded within their Shots. Silence is used after moral challenges and completed harm. |
| Editorial Rhythm | The opening is patient, Lion's ascent uses increasingly compressed cuts, violence lands without montage ambiguity, Mosquito's challenge alternates macro detail with spatial consequence, and the ending restores longer holds. Cuts occur only after a visible state change or on an explicitly unfinished action continued inside the same Generation Segment. |
| Global Constraints | Every Segment is subtitle-free, logo-free, and watermark-free. Preserve identities and established states; do not invent characters, dialogue, narration, or actions. Segment rows below state only additional local risks. |
